Sketch #1 -- Echo (Named after echolocation)

IDEA:

  • This little watch like device (potentially even just a smart watch app) would help people with hearing impairments.
  • The device would constantly listen to the environment for a pre-programmed keyphrase. In this case, a name.
  • The device listens to the environment with its microphone. Using some sound-analysis algo, it determines if that keyphrase is said.
  • If the phrase is said, it starts it's vibration motors and creates a vibration pointing in the direction of the keyphrase audio's location.
  • The end result is a person who can get a sensory input they may not have had without the device.

AUDIENCE:

  • Hearing impaired individuals.

HARDWARE REQUIRED:

  • Vibration Motor(s)
  • Microphone
  • Small screen
  • Wristband, some electrical board...

SIGNIFIERS:

  • Some kind of waveform animation to denote that the device is listening.
  • A directional arrow displayed on screen in the direction of a matched keyphrase. This indicates direction in addition to the vibrations.

POSSIBLE EXTENSIONS:

  • Implement this for the blind to listen for loud noises moving the direction of the device
  • This kind of echo location can help the blind avoid incoming cars for instance

Assignment 2

There are 2 circuits in parallel. They both have 5V going through them and 100 ohm resistors. This means the current flowing through each subcircuit is .05A and in total there is 1A in the circuit.
